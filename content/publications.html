Publications

realfast has been supported via NSF awards NSF awards 2022546 (ATI, PI: Law) and 1714897 (AAG, PI: Burke-Spolaor). Early development supported by the University of California Lab Fees Research Program.

Technical development: 1. Law et al. (2015) describes a prototype realfast system that searched 200 hours and 200 TB of data for FRBs. 1. Law et al. (2018) is the realfast project paper. 2. Pokorny et al. (2018) describes the high-speed data distribution protocol.

Science using realfast: 1. Chatterjee et al. (2017) describes how a realfast prototype made the first interferometric localization and host identification of an FRB (see image below). 1. Law et al. (2017) present a detailed analysis of radio bursts from FRB 121102. 1. Law et al. (2019) presents a simultaneous fast/slow transient survey targeting SLSN. 1. Marcote et al. (2020) describes the localization of an FRB by the EVN and includes a deep image from commensal VLA/realfast observations. 1. TBD
